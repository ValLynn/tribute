Bessie Coleman

Lady of the Sky

Who Was Bessie Coleman

Bessie Coleman was an American aviator. She was the first internationally licensed Indigenous female pilot and the first internationally licensed African-American woman to earn a pilot license.

Timeline Highlights

  • 1892 - Born in Atlanta, Texas to George and Susan Coleman; raised in Waxahachie, Texas.
  • 1915 - Moved to Chicago at 23 years old
  • 1916 - Worked in Cosmetology and as a Manicurist, winning a contest for the best and fastest manicurist in Chicago, Illinois.
  • 1917 - Married Claude Glenn, a friend of her brother, Walter. They never lived together.
  • 1919 - Enrolled in French language class at Berlitz in Chicago; quit working as a manicurist and began working as a manager in a "Chill Parlor."
  • 1920 - Boarded a ship in New York bound for France intent on finding a flight school.
  • 1920 - Began Flight Training at Ecole d' Aviation des Freres Caudron at Le Crotoy (Northern France).
  • 1921 - Received her International Pilot License from Federation Aeronautique Internationale.
  • 1922 - First Indigenous and African American female to perform at an air show (New York, Labor Day).
  • 1922 - First Indigenous and African American female to perform at an air show (New York, Labor Day).
  • 1923 - Hospitalized after crash in Santa Monica, CA. The engine of her Curtis JN-4 quit after takeoff at 300 feet.
  • 1926 - April 27, While looking for a suitable parachute-jumping location before an airshow, Bessie Coleman's airplane enters a tailspin. She is thrown out at 500 feet above the ground and tumbles to her death. The airplane, flown by her mechanic / pilot, William Wills, crashes, also killing Wills.
